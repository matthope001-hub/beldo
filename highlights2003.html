Berby Highlights & Notes - 2003

A look back at the 2003 season

Season Overview

The 2003 season was one of the most competitive and memorable years in Berby history. With the core group of players at their peak and rivalries heating up, every game brought intense competition and unforgettable moments.

Key Moments

The Summer Classic

One of the standout games of the season took place during the height of summer. The game went into extra innings with both teams trading runs back and forth. The final score was decided by a walk-off home run that cleared the center field fence.

The Rivalry Intensifies

2003 saw the rivalries reach new levels. With stats being tracked more meticulously than ever, every at-bat mattered. Players were pushing themselves to improve their batting averages and slugging percentages.

Record Breaking Performances

Several records were challenged and broken during the 2003 season:

  • Most home runs in a single game
  • Highest batting average over a 10-game stretch
  • Most RBIs in a season
  • Longest hitting streak

Player Notes

Consistent Performers

The core group continued to show why they've been the foundation of Berby for so many years. Their dedication to the game and the stats they keep has made every season better than the last.

New Faces

A few outsiders joined the games throughout the season and quickly realized why Berby has become such a beloved tradition. While some became regulars, others made memorable appearances that added to the season's stories.

Field Conditions

St. Helen's School in Hamilton continued to serve as the home of Berby. The diamond at the school saw most of the action throughout the 2003 season. The fences provided their usual challenges and opportunities, with players learning to use the field's quirks to their advantage. A few games were also played at Hillsdale/Hill Crest Schools.

Looking Forward

As the 2003 season came to a close, excitement was already building for the next year. With more stats to track and rivalries continuing to develop, the future of Berby looked bright.
