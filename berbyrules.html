Berby Rules & Regulations

As of July 07, 2003

Introduction

Berby was formed by a couple of friends who didn't have much to do. They started to go play Berby just for fun. After a while it got boring because they were just playing. There wasn't much competition. So those guys decided to keep stats. As the friends got older, and realizing the potential of Berby, they added more and more stats. The competition of today is fierce. Never has the rivalries been more intense. Everyone once in a while an outsider will join, and realize how much fun Berby is. Over the years, only the main core of guys has played continuously. To this day, those core guys still play. The older they get, the more stats they find to keep, and the more competition it is. Here are the latest rules that we have for Berby. Enjoy, and have fun!

RULE 1: The Game, Field, Equipment, & Statistics

The Game

Berby is made up of 3 or more players. Once there are more than 3 players, they are divided up into teams.

The Field

All Berby games are played at St. Helen's School in Hamilton, with occasional games at Hillsdale/Hill Crest Schools. The diamond specifications and fence rules are agreed upon by all players before each game. The fence in left field is an automatic triple. Right field fence is an automatic double. The middle fence is a double, however a ground ball through the fence is a single. Center field fence is a triple. A ball hit over any fence is an automatic home run.

Equipment

All games will be played with a hard ball, or a "pimple" ball. If in agreement, a tennis ball may be used. But all players must agree to use a tennis ball. Aluminum bats will be used. If there are not enough bats, wood or composite bats may be used.

Statistics

Statistics are kept for every game. Individual stats include:

  • At Bats (AB)
  • Runs (R)
  • Hits (H)
  • Doubles (2B)
  • Triples (3B)
  • Home Runs (HR)
  • Runs Batted In (RBI)
  • Batting Average (AVG)
  • Slugging Percentage (SLG)
  • Errors

RULE 2: Game Play

Innings

Each game is 9 innings long. If the game is tied after 9 innings, extra innings will be played until there is a winner. All players must bat. If there are more than 3 players, the teams will be divided up as evenly as possible.

Outs

Three outs per inning. Outs can be made by:

  • Catching a fly ball
  • Throwing a runner out at a base
  • Tagging a runner
  • Striking out (if pitching is being used)

Scoring

A run is scored when a player crosses home plate. The team with the most runs at the end of the game wins.

RULE 3: Special Rules

The "Ghost Runner"

If a player has to leave early, or is injured, they may use a "ghost runner." The ghost runner will advance the same number of bases as the batter. For example, if the batter hits a single, the ghost runner advances to second base.

Home Run Limit

There is no home run limit in Berby. Hit as many as you can!

Mercy Rule

If a team is up by 10 runs after 5 innings, the game may be called by agreement of all players.

RULE 4: Conduct

All players are expected to play fair and have fun. Arguments will be settled by discussion. The commissioner has final say in all disputes.
