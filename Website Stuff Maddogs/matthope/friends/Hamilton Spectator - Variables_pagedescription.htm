Go bigger, go Pugger
Lori Fazari
The Hamilton Spectator
John Rennison, the Hamilton Spectator

Tongdee Khamphoomee tucks into the popular one-pound Pugger burger at O'Neill's.

In the next few weeks we'll take you on a spin around some classic quick eats near and far, paying tribute to the hotdogs and hamburgers, the fish and chips, the milkshakes and sundaes of beach weather

I'm scared of the Pugger burger.

It's one pound (450 g) of ground meat, shaped into a hamburger patty. Raw, it's the size of a dinner plate.

Sure, it shrinks when it's cooked, enough to be reasonably contained by a five-inch (13-cm) kaiser bun. But then it's topped with thick slices of bacon and cheddar cheese, and served alongside onion rings.

That makes for one mammoth meal.

A hefty load to lift from takeout box to mouth, it's not something to jump into lightly.

I've seen the Pugger, and I'll be a wuss and settle for the half-pound burger any day, itself nearly an inch (2.5 cm) of meat on a bun.

There are many people whose appetites are up to the Pugger challenge.

They sell a few dozen a day at O'Neill's, which owner Vanh Bouly Kalong likes to call the hometown hamburger joint.

It's celebrating its 20th year in business on Barton Street, and the Pugger has stuck around more than half that time.

A friend of the former owner used to special-order a one-pound burger when he came to town. His nickname was Pugger, and his burger eventually found a home at O'Neill's.

The menu stretches across two pages, but it's the Pugger that's painted on a mural outside the building.

The burgers go on the grill right when you order them, shaped by hand and made with nothing but ground beef seasoned with diced onion and garlic powder, salt and pepper.

The milkshakes are thick -- really thick -- and the fries wide and golden.

O'Neill's offers takeout and delivery only. Behind the counter, you'll spot delivery info for Dofasco and Stelco and National Steel Car.

Those diners obviously know their stuff, throwing their lunchtime support behind a good Pugger of a burger over an invisible fast-food patty.
