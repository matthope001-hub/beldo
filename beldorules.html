Beldo's Berby Rules For Hitting

Official Batting Rules and Guidelines

Stance and Preparation

The batter must stand within the designated batter's box. Both feet must be positioned appropriately - one foot can be on the line, but not completely outside the box. The batter may take practice swings before the pitch is delivered.

The Swing

A complete swing must be taken. Bunting or half-swings are not permitted in Beldo play. The batter must attempt to hit the ball with a full, committed swing.

Fair and Foul Balls

Fair Balls

  • A ball that lands in fair territory is a fair ball
  • A ball that hits first or third base is fair
  • A ball that passes over first or third base in fair territory is fair
  • A ball that hits the foul pole is a home run

Foul Balls

  • A ball that lands in foul territory is a foul ball
  • A ball that hits the batter in the batter's box is foul
  • A ball that goes out of play in foul territory is foul

Strikes

A strike is called when:

  • The batter swings and misses
  • The ball is hit foul (with less than 2 strikes)
  • The pitch is in the strike zone and the batter doesn't swing

The batter is out on the third strike.

Balls and Walks

If pitching is being used, four balls result in a walk. The batter advances to first base. If first base is occupied, that runner also advances (forced walk).

Hit Types

Single

A hit that allows the batter to reach first base safely without any errors by the defense.

Double

A hit that allows the batter to reach second base safely. Ground ball through the middle fence is an automatic double.

Triple

A hit that allows the batter to reach third base safely. The left field fence at St. Helen's School is an automatic triple.

Home Run

A ball hit over any fence in fair territory is a home run. Inside-the-park home runs are also possible if the batter can circle all bases before being tagged or thrown out.

Special Situations

Infield Fly Rule

When there are runners on first and second (or bases loaded) with less than 2 outs, and a pop-up is hit to the infield, the batter is automatically out. This prevents the defense from dropping the ball to get a double play.

Dropped Third Strike

If the catcher drops the third strike, the batter may attempt to run to first base. However, this only applies if first base is unoccupied or there are 2 outs.

Base Running

Runners must touch each base in order. If a runner misses a base, they must go back and touch it before advancing. Runners can be put out by:

  • Being tagged while not on a base
  • Being forced out at the next base
  • Passing another runner

Scoring

A run is scored when a runner legally touches home plate. Runs can be driven in by hits, walks, sacrifice flies, or errors by the defense.

Play hard, play fair, have fun!
