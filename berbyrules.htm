Berby Rules & Regulations

As of July 07, 2003

 

Introduction

 

        Berby was formed by a couple of friends who didn't have much to do.  They started to go play Berby just for fun.  After a while it got boring because they were just playing.  There wasn't much competition.  So those guys decided to keep stats.  As the friends got older, and realizing the potential of Berby, they added more and more stats.  The competition of today is fierce.  Never has the rivalries been more intense. Everyone once in a while an outside will join, and realize how much fun Berby is. Over the years, only the main core of guys has played continuously.  To this day, those core guys still play.  The older they get, the more stats they find to keep, and the more competition it is.  Here the 'latest' rules that we have for Berby.  Enjoy, and have fun! 

RULE 1

The Game, Field, Equipment, & Statistics

            The Game

            Berby is made up of 3 of more players.  Once there are more than 3 players, they are divided up into teams. 

            For a 3-player game of Berby to start, there must be 1 batter, 1 pitcher, and 1 outfielder.  The batters objective is to have base runners advance to score runs.  The pitchers objective is to not let the batter score runs.  The outfielders objective is to help the pitcher prevent runs from being scored.  The object of the game is to score more runs than your opponents.  That person who has the best +/- at the end of the regulation 5-inning game wins.   The winner will get a win, the loser will be marked as the person who pitched to the winner, and the third player will get a draw, as a no decision because they didn’t no win nor lose, but still must be counted in the game. 

For a 4 or more game of Berby to start, one team calls going up, the other is outfield and pitching.  At bat, the team must alternate taking at bats.  While the fielding team must have each player pitch at least 1 out.  The object of the game is to score more runs than your opponents.  The team who has the most amount of runs at the end of the regulation 5 inning game wins.  

            If for any reason there is a tie at the end of the regulation 5 innings, extra innings will be played until a winner is determined.

                The Equipment

Over the years we have tried numerous balls, from tennis to racquet to Ikea kids balls, and we have found from out experience that the most fun balls to use are the Ikea balls.  They don't have too much bounce in them like the racquet balls do, and they are livelier than a tennis ball.  A wooden bat MAY be used, but the preferred bat of Berby is an aluminum bat.  Typical baseball gloves maybe worn.  

 

                Statistics Keeping

Each team is responsible for keeping their stats up to date and accurate while up to bat.  The team or player up to bat may also mark stats down for pitching and out fielding.  If an error is found it is to be corrected immediately. The stats MUST be written neatly so that the commish can easily see what the stats are.  If it is hard to recognize what it is, the commish will use the best judgment on what it supposed to be.  The complete game stats go to the commish as soon as he is able to get them so that the stats can be updated.   If a game goes incomplete for any reason, stats are retained and game MAY be continued on next available day.  At the end of the season, all stats are counted, even those games that are inconclusive. 

 

RULE 2

Pitching, Batting, & Fielding

 

            Pitching

            As in baseball we have the 3 strikes you’re out, 4 balls walk, and 3 outs rule for an inning. The pitcher is to start at the designated pitching line and to throw into the box on the wall.  If it is in the box, a strike is called, if it is outside the box, a ball is called, If it is on the line of the box, a line may be called, which in turn is just a re pitch.  If there is an dispute between the batter and the pitcher on the call, the outfielder may be the 3rd party to make a decision on the call, if they are unable to do so, then a re-pitch is also possible.  When a ball is hit to the outfield and the outfielder is able to make a relay throw in, the pitcher may get into position along the pitching line to do the throw in to get the batter out, without stepping OVER the line.  If the pitcher is able to get the ball into the box on the throw in, the batter is out.  The pitcher may throw, bounce whatever is needed, but they are not allowed over the line.  The pitcher is also allowed to offer foul balls on balls that might come back from a rebound off a tree or such.

 

            Hitting

            Just as in baseball the batter has the 3-strike count he’s out, 4 balls they walk, and the typical 3 outs per inning.   The base hits are as follows:  A ground ball is considered a single, unless A. It doesn’t pass the pitcher, B. it goes foul.  A double on a hit would be called if the ball were hit over the head of the outfielder.  A triple would be called if it hits the homerun fence before it hits the ground, and a homerun is if the ball is hit over the fence.  Certain circumstances result to these hits:  A ground rule single is hit, and the ball finds it way to the other side of the fence either by going through the fence, or the opening.  A ground rule double is if the ball bounces over the fence, and a ground rule triple is if the ball is hit in air to the fence and the ball goes through the fence.  IF the ball can be retrieved from the other side by reaching under or through the fence, it is considered a live ball and the batter can be thrown out.   If the ball is IN play, and a NON-player touches the ball, the ball is marked dead, and the hit is not allowed, that at bat is redone.  The batter also must be able to see where the ball lands for it to be fair.  If it is not fair, it is foul ball and considered a strike.  If the ball hits the tree and comes back into foul territory the batter can request a foul ball, but the pitcher does NOT have to give a foul ball, the trees are considered in.  If the ball hits a tree that is on the other side of the fence but comes back into play BEFORE it hits the ground, the ball is considered live, and the batter can be thrown out.  If the outfielder can get the ball before it goes off the concrete but it has to be fair, the batter will be called out.
