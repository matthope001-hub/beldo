How to Play Texas Hold'em

The most popular poker game in the world

Introduction

Texas Hold'em is a variation of the card game of poker. Two cards, known as hole cards, are dealt face down to each player, and then five community cards are dealt face up in three stages. The stages consist of a series of three cards ("the flop"), later an additional single card ("the turn" or "fourth street"), and a final card ("the river" or "fifth street"). Each player seeks the best five-card poker hand from any combination of the seven cards: the five community cards and their two hole cards.

The Basics

Number of Players

Texas Hold'em can be played with 2 to 10 players. The game works best with 6-9 players.

The Deck

A standard 52-card deck is used. No jokers or wild cards.

Objective

Win the pot by having the best hand at showdown, or by getting all other players to fold before showdown.

Gameplay

1. Blinds

Before cards are dealt, the two players to the left of the dealer post forced bets called blinds:

  • Small Blind: Usually half the minimum bet
  • Big Blind: Equal to the minimum bet

2. Hole Cards

Each player receives two private cards face down. These are your hole cards - only you can see them.

3. First Betting Round (Pre-Flop)

Starting with the player to the left of the big blind, each player can:

  • Call: Match the big blind
  • Raise: Increase the bet
  • Fold: Discard hand and wait for next deal

4. The Flop

Three community cards are dealt face up in the center of the table. These cards are shared by all players.

5. Second Betting Round

Another round of betting occurs, starting with the player to the dealer's left. Players can check (pass the action) if no bet has been made.

6. The Turn

A fourth community card is dealt face up.

7. Third Betting Round

Another betting round takes place with the same options as before.

8. The River

The fifth and final community card is dealt face up.

9. Final Betting Round

The last chance to bet before showdown.

10. Showdown

If two or more players remain after the final betting round, they reveal their hole cards. The player with the best five-card hand wins the pot.

Hand Rankings (Best to Worst)

  1. Royal Flush: A, K, Q, J, 10, all same suit
  2. Straight Flush: Five consecutive cards, same suit
  3. Four of a Kind: Four cards of same rank
  4. Full House: Three of a kind plus a pair
  5. Flush: Five cards, same suit
  6. Straight: Five consecutive cards, any suits
  7. Three of a Kind: Three cards of same rank
  8. Two Pair: Two different pairs
  9. One Pair: Two cards of same rank
  10. High Card: Highest single card

Betting Actions

  • Bet: Place chips in the pot
  • Call: Match the current bet
  • Raise: Increase the current bet
  • Check: Pass action (only if no bet made)
  • Fold: Surrender your hand
  • All-In: Bet all remaining chips

Basic Strategy Tips

  • Play tight in early position, looser in late position
  • Be selective with starting hands
  • Pay attention to position - acting last is a big advantage
  • Don't chase draws unless the pot odds justify it
  • Watch your opponents for betting patterns
  • Manage your bankroll wisely
  • Know when to fold - not every hand is worth playing

Common Terms

  • Pot: The total amount bet in a hand
  • Chips: Tokens representing money
  • Dealer Button: Marker showing who deals
  • Kicker: Highest unpaired card
  • Nuts: Best possible hand
  • Draw: Hand needing one more card to improve
  • Bluff: Betting with a weak hand to make opponents fold
  • Position: Your seat relative to the dealer

Etiquette

  • Don't discuss your hand while others are still playing
  • Keep your cards on the table
  • Act in turn - don't slow down the game
  • Be respectful to other players
  • Don't splash the pot when betting

Good luck at the tables!
